Getting Started

Your First Search

  1. Type your question or claim in the search box
  2. Click "Search" or press Enter
  3. Wait for AI to analyze and find sources
  4. Review the fact-check results and sources
  5. Check the credibility rating and explanations
Pro Tip: Be specific with your questions. Instead of "Is coffee healthy?", try "What are the health benefits of drinking 2 cups of coffee daily?"

Understanding Results

Accuracy Score

Shows the percentage of sources that support or contradict the claim. Green = mostly true, Red = mostly false.

Source Credibility

Indicates how reliable the sources are. Look for trusted domains like .gov, .edu, and reputable news sites.

Source Count

Number of sources analyzed. More sources generally mean more reliable conclusions.

Search Tips

  • Use complete sentences rather than keywords
  • Include specific dates, places, or people
  • Avoid yes/no questions - ask for explanations instead
  • Be neutral and objective in your phrasing

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is VeriFact?

VeriFact achieves high accuracy by analyzing multiple credible sources and using AI to identify consensus. However, no fact-checking system is 100% perfect. Always consider multiple sources and use critical thinking.

What sources does VeriFact use?

VeriFact searches across thousands of sources including news outlets, academic journals, government publications, and reputable websites. Sources are weighted by credibility and relevance to your query.

Is my search history private?

Yes, your search history is completely private and only accessible to you. We don't share your searches with third parties, and you can delete your history at any time.

How can I improve my search results?

Be specific, use complete sentences, include relevant context like dates or locations, and avoid biased language. The more neutral and specific your query, the better the results.

What do the credibility ratings mean?

Credibility ratings are based on domain authority, editorial standards, expertise, and accuracy history. Trusted sources (.gov, .edu, major news outlets) rate higher than blogs or opinion sites.

Can I use VeriFact for academic research?

VeriFact is great for initial research and fact-checking, but always verify sources directly for academic work. Use it as a starting point to find credible sources, then examine those sources yourself.

How often are sources updated?

VeriFact searches the live web each time you query, so you get the most current information available. Source credibility scores are updated regularly based on ongoing analysis.

What should I do if I find incorrect information?

Please use the feedback feature to report any issues. We continuously improve our algorithms and source analysis based on user feedback.
